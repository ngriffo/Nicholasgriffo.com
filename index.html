ABOUT

Film is decisions made one frame at a time. AI is the same decisions made at scale.

I started as a camera assistant on NYC productions, learning that the gap between a good shot and a great one is always craft — not budget. That instinct followed me into production design, building sets and environments that had to feel real enough to disappear. The invisible work is the hardest. It's also the most valuable.

Today, as an independent contractor, I provide creative and visual expertise to help improve generative AI models for a leading AI lab. My job draws directly on six years of production design and art direction — knowing instantly when something is almost right but isn't. That instinct is exactly where I live.

That kind of instinct doesn't come from a course. It comes from standing on enough sets where being wrong cost real money, and learning to trust the gut call before you can explain it.

WHY FILM → AI

What I Did On Set What That Means For AI Why It Matters
Production Design → World-building for generative models — defining what a scene should contain, feel, and imply. AI video tools need creative directors who can articulate visual logic, not just taste.
Continuity & Coherence → Detecting spatial and temporal errors in generated outputs that break viewer trust. The uncanny valley is a craft problem. Film trains the eye to close it.
Directing → Prompt engineering, creative direction, and defining the intent behind a generation. The best prompt is a director's shot list. Most AI teams don't have a director.
Multi-Location Management → Managing pipelines across multiple models, teams, and output types simultaneously. Scale on a film set and scale in a model pipeline require identical coordination instincts.
Budget & Sourcing → Resource allocation, vendor evaluation, and creative problem-solving under constraint. Startups need people who maximize output with minimum waste. That's every film.
Shooting on 16mm → High-stakes, low-margin-for-error decision-making. No undo button. Permanent decisions build better judgment than infinite iteration.
